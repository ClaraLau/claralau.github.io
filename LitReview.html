The Relationship Between Web Accessibility and Usability

A Literature Review

Home > User Research Projects >

Project Overview

Dates: May- August 2020 (Submitted as a fulfillment for SYDE 644 User-Centered Testing Methods)
Description: Usability and Accessibility are terms that are often used together in Web design but how are they similar and how do they differ? Does improving accessibility also improve usability? This literature review examines publications including compartiive and empirical studies to answer these questions and further provides insights on how usability and accessibility testing methods can be applied together in industry to greatly improve the user experience on websites.

Methods Used:
  • Literature Review
  • Quantitative and Qualitative Studies

    • Conclusion

  • The literature generally supports that web accessibility and usability are positively related and that improving conformance with accessibility guidelines can lead to benefits in usability for all users regardless of disability and age
  • Investing in proper accessibility evaluations can help software development teams and businesses create websites that are not only accessible for people with disabilities but websites that also improve the user experience for all users
  • The interrelated nature of web accessibility and usability also suggests that it may be possible to develop integrated measures to combine measurements for accessibility and usability to find overlapping issues
  • Certain literature sources are concerned about the limitations of relying on evaluation methods that only focus on compliance to accessibility standards. These sources argue that accessibility guidelines have limited validity and urge researchers to adopt research approaches that involve more representative users and to explore the social and cultural nuances of web experiences of people with disabilities.
