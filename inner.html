Child's Play

What is Child's Play?

Child's Play is an Adventure RPG created by 7 Deadly Sins at the Fall 2014 GI Game Jam held at uWaterloo.
The game can be found Here.

Game Concept

What was just the heroine's backyward becomes a mystical forest. Worn out clothes become armor and everyday items are now your weapons,
Advance and clear the trials that await you!

Game Jam Themes:
Unorthodox Main Character & Symmetry
The concept was to create a game where the player can embark on an quest through the eyes children whose imaginative perceptions of the world are assymetrical to how the world objectively appears.

Hopefully it will remind you of all those times you ran around playing make-believe as a child :)

What I Contributed

As one of the Graphic Artists on the team, I mainly worked alongside the other artist to generate character designs and create the character sprites and other visual assets. In particular, I spent a good amount of time working on the sprites for the heroine:

The Game Jam Process

After forming our team (3 Programmers, 2 Designers, 1 Composer and 1 person for Moral Support), we rapidly got to work brainstorming game and character ideas.

Tools Involved:


Throughout the Jam we wrote over the walls of QNC and on whiteboards to flesh out ideas, we stored most of the assets in a common Dropbox and worked collaboratively in the limited time we had.




A lot of interesting ideas came up (even though we weren't able to implement them all) but in particular my favourites are:
  • The character's all have 2 separate designs: 1 for their normal appearance and 1 alternate for how they imagine themselves to be
  • The Shop owner who sells you items along the way is actually.. the final boss
  • The added twist is: he fights you with everything you didn't buy!
  • The hidden final is actually the heroine's mother calling them all to go home, unfortunately you can't win. Cue the credits!

    ConnectTap

    What is ConnectTap?


    ConnectTap is a Phone App Business Idea created by a group of Business students from the University of Waterloo and University of Laurier. It is essentially a networking app that allows users to create and exchange digital business cards through wireless technology built into smart phones.

    What did I do?

    I volunteered to assist the group of Business students in creating an interactive prototype that would showcase the plausability and benefits of their business idea.
    Tools Used:
    Details on the project I was given:
  • The purpose of the app ( to exchange digital business cards)
  • The audience (people who are active networkers)
  • A logo design
    • What I created:
  • Screen designs in the form of simple wireframes
  • An interactive prototype viewable on a phone or on a laptop

    • Process

    I started by generating a list of required functions.
    Functions


  • Enable the user to create a digital business card
  • Record the users profession and contact information
  • Allow users to delete and manage cards they have made in the past
  • Allow user to exchange digital business cards with other people
  • Provide a means for the user to organize their contacts
  • Enable the user to easily retrieve exchanged contact information

    • Wireframes

    I then brainstormed possible screens that would be needed and generated some simple wireframes





    Interactive Prototype

    The last step I took in this project was to refine screens and create an interactive prototype using Axure RP.
    A demo of the prototype is showcased in the video below:

    Areas of Improvement

    I took on this initiative because I wanted to become more familiar with Axure RP and the wireframing process in general. Overall, due to time constraints I ended up using many pre-made icons and graphics. In the future, for similar projects I would like to challenge myself to create my own icons. Next, through this project I was able to realize the importance of screen space allocation and how the interactable space is quite limited on phones. Additionally, I feel I could have spent more time on the idea generation and brainstorming alternative screens/ solutions. To me, this project was a good starting point to understand the design process for the UI of phone applications. A next step in this project would probably be to further refine the graphics and perhaps even try to redesign some of the screens from scratch.

    Nest Thermostat Case Study

    In our Human Factors in Design course, we completed a Usability project where performed various Usability assessments on a product with physical controls and a visual display.The product my group chose was the Nest Thermostat; a programmable thermostat which is a great example of a Smart Home innovation.(Photo from: store.nest.com)

    What was done?

    The project was executed in the form of multiple design sprints. Some of the activities include:
  • Brainstorming User Requirements
  • Creating Personas
  • Current Design Sketches
  • Hierarchial Task Analysis and Fault Tree Analysis
  • Heuristic Evaluation
  • A Mock Usability Test
  • Display and Controls Analysis
  • Proposing a Re-Design

    • What did I contribute

    Along with participating in all of the design prints, one of the main things I contributed (in terms of project deliverables) was the sketches for the current design and proposed redesign.
